Why I Built ME

My name is Kent Stone. I'm a U.S. Army veteran who served with the 10th Mountain Division in Iraq. When I came home, I didn't bring back medals or clarity—I brought back trauma, chronic illness, and questions no one could answer.

Years later, after being diagnosed with ankylosing spondylitis and spinal stenosis, my world shrank to pain management and persistence. I tracked symptoms, doctors' dismissals, medications, flare-ups—but the tools out there weren't made for people like me. They were made for wellness influencers, not warriors managing pain.

I didn't need affirmations. I needed accountability, pattern recognition, and evidence—something that could help me advocate for myself when my body and doctors stopped listening.

"So I started coding. What began as a late-night side project on a broken laptop in Peru evolved into ME—a first-of-its-kind journaling platform designed for chronic illness survivors, veterans, and trauma fighters."

Not just another "mindfulness" app—but a data-driven healing companion that helps people document, understand, and reclaim their lives. Where most apps treat reflection like a habit, I treat it like a vital sign.

I built this because I was tired of being invisible. Because thousands of people—veterans, patients, caregivers—are living in the quiet corners of pain, tracking symptoms in notebooks and screenshots, unheard.

ME exists so they don't have to. Because reflection isn't selfish—it's survival.
