The Approach

A commissioned piece, not a photo package

Every wedding is photographed with the same restraint and intention as a fine art commission — unobtrusive on the day, and considered down to the single image that becomes the artwork on your wall.

Kent Stone

Not a weekend hobbyist. A National Geographic-funded explorer.

U.S. Army veteran, founder of the BBC-featured Wonderland Project, and a photographer who has worked across 74 countries. Every wedding on the calendar is photographed personally — never handed to an associate — with the same technical discipline built photographing Mexico's cave systems in total darkness. That same background in photogrammetry and 3D scanning is what makes the preservation side of this work possible — not just documenting your day, but archiving it.

The Craft

Built for the once-in-a-lifetime

The Museum Print

A single archival print, 3×4 to 4×6 feet, on pigment-ink fine-art paper with 100+ year permanence — designed to outlive both of us, not to sit on a hard drive.

The Legacy Book

120 curated photographs, not every image — a hardcover, lay-flat volume built to be held, passed down, and still look extraordinary in fifty years.

The Preservation Archive

Color-managed digital negatives prepared for decades of reprinting, with an optional 3D-scan add-on for rings, bouquets, and keepsakes — preserving more than the photographs.
